Preliminary Invocation and Applicant Orientation Protocol

Welcome, esteemed finder, witness, or accidental discoverer of contextually relevant phenomena. Before you stands Form A-7/14b, the Department’s officially semi-sanctioned mechanism for reporting artefacts, anomalies, and everyday occurrences whose significance is—at the moment—unverified, unbelievable, or merely suspected by you.

As a participant in this administrative rite, you are about to enter a three-phase labyrinth of procedural introspection and bureaucratic self-revelation. Each section has been meticulously designed to assess not only what you have found, but who you become while attempting to report it.

Applicants are reminded that submitting this form does not guarantee review, recognition, acknowledgement, or even visibility. Your submission will, however, be archived indefinitely in the Vault of Pending Interpretations, where it may one day be examined by a future intern with a minor in Hermeneutic Risk Assessment.

Proceed, if you dare. And remember: Meaning is mandatory. Understanding is optional.
